Types of Accounting Jobs


  1. Financial accounting

    • Financial accounting is a specific branch of accounting involving gathering data for financial reports for external reporting.
    • Financial accountants collaborate with their colleagues and managers to devise strategies for increasing a company's profitability.
    • Financial accountants monitor all financial activity recorded in a ledger and ensure that internal procedures are followed and that all financial activity appears on relevant financial statements. Companies will then use these financial statements to show their financial performance and position.

  2. Managerial accounting

    • Managerial accounting is a specific branch of accounting involving documenting, monitoring, and assisting in the financial planning of an organization.
    • Managerial accountants collaborate with their managers to analyze and develop a budget to meet the organization's short- and long-term objectives.
    • Managerial accountants are seeking to evaluate past performance in order to forecast future performance.

  3. Cost accounting

    • Cost accounting is a subcategory of managerial accounting which involves documenting, presenting, and reviewing manufacturing costs.
    • Cost accountants supervise all fixed and variable costs to ensure that output matches the cost of producing a product.
    • Cost accountants can determine how much it costs to employ them versus how much they bring in.
    • Cost accountants collaborate with managers to make future decisions based on financial projections and production progress.

  4. Auditing

    • Auditing is conducted by a Certified Public Accountant in a company that sends financial documents to a third party for financial analysis.
    • Auditors oversee employee departmental responsibilities, management policies, and project approval procedures can all be reviewed by auditors.
    • Auditors provide valuable feedback that can assist a company in becoming more profitable and efficient.

  5. Tax accounting

    • Tax accounting involves the process of assisting companies to file their tax returns each year and help them remain in compliance with the Internal Revenue Code.
    • Tax accountants also help businesses plan for future tax returns, such as avoiding certain tax burdens and comprehending the consequences of specific tax decisions.
    • Tax accountants are typically hired by larger organizations to help them navigate the complexities of financial records.

  6. Forensic accounting

    • Forensic accounting is used to investigate individuals' or businesses' financial records, and it may necessitate accountants recreating financial information when some information is missing or unavailable for review.
    • Forensic accounting aims to collect all available documentation and account for all transactions in financial statements accurately and comprehensively.
    • Forensic accountants frequently work on legal cases involving fraud, claims, and disputes

  7. Public accounting

    • Public accounting involves a company or business providing accounting advice to clients based on their needs.
    • Public accountants can work in auditing, help with tax returns, consult on procedures for installing technology or computer programs, and provide legal advice.

  8. Governmental accounting

    • Governmental accountants oversee financial planning and resource allocation to local, state, or federal government departments.
    • Governmental accountants must adhere to standards established by the Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB), which is in charge of developing consistent accounting procedures for local and state governments.
    • Governmental accountants will keep track of a government's budget and allocate funds as needed.
